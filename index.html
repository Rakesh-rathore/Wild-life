The Oryx of Desert

oryx is a genus of four large antelope species native to Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. They have pale fur with dark markings on the face and legs, and long, straight or slightly curved horns. The four species are the Arabian oryx, the scimitar oryx, the East African oryx, and the gemsbok. The Arabian oryx was extinct in the wild until it was reintroduced in 1980, and the scimitar oryx is now listed as possibly extinct in the wild12.

Zebra of Africa

zebra is a subgenus of African equines with distinctive black-and-white striped coats. There are three living species: the Grévy’s zebra, the plains zebra, and the mountain zebra. Zebras share the genus Equus with horses and asses, but have different social behavior and adaptations

The sunrise Deer

Deer are a group of hoofed ruminant mammals belonging to the family Cervidae. They are notable for having two large and two small hooves on each foot and antlers in most males and some females. There are about 60 species of deer native to various continents, except Australia and Antarctica. Some of the well-known species are the white-tailed deer, the red deer, the moose, and the reindeer. Deer are herbivorous and have a variety of adaptations for different habitats and seasons.

A lone Bison

Bison are a genus of large bovids that belong to the tribe Bovini. There are two extant species of bison: the American bison (Bison bison), also known as the buffalo, and the European bison (Bison bonasus), also known as the wisent. Bison have shaggy fur, humped shoulders, and curved horns. They are herbivorous and live in herds. Bison were once widespread and abundant in North America and Europe, but they were hunted to near extinction in the 19th and 20th centuries. Now they are protected and confined to small reserves and parks.

Flamingos is a type of wading bird in the family Phoenicopteridae, which is the only family in the order Phoenicopteriformes. There are six species of flamingos distributed throughout Africa, Asia, Europe, and the Americas. Flamingos are famous for their bright pink feathers, stilt-like legs, and S-shaped necks. They have thick downturned bills that they use to filter food from the water. Flamingos are highly gregarious and form large flocks that can number in the thousands or even millions

Bears of North America

There are three species of bears that live in North America: black bears, brown bears, and polar bears.
Brown bears are also known as grizzly bears or Kodiak bears, depending on their location and size. They are found in British Columbia, Alberta, the Yukon and Northwest territories in Canada, and in Wyoming, Montana, Idaho, Washington, and Alaska in the United States. They have brown fur with lighter tips, giving them a grizzled appearance. They also have a distinctive hump on their shoulders. They are mostly omnivorous, but some coastal populations feed mainly on salmon. They are more aggressive than black bears and will defend their food and territory.
